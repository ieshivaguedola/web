Rab. Osher Farkash

General Director (Menahel) of Yeshiva
General Mashpia

Born in Jerusalem, he began his Talmud Torah studies in his hometown at Chaye Olam and Torat Emet Chabad. After his Bar Mitzvah, he moved to the Mesivte of Tomchei Tmimim in Montreal, Canada, and later joined Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires, all over a period of 5 years. During the holidays, he traveled to New York, to the Chabad central 770, to be close to the Lubavitch Rebbe.
In 1990 (5750), he had the merit and honor to travel to Australia, where he spent two years on Shlichut, strengthening the Yeshiva and the local community. In 1992 (5752), he returned to New York, where he studied for two years at 770 in the central Tomchei Tmimim Yeshiva, very close to the Rebbe. His studies continued in the U.S. until his graduation (Smicha) as a Rabbi. In 1994 (5754), he began serving as Mashpia at the Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires for two years.
After his marriage, he moved to Buenos Aires to accept his position as General Director and Mashpia of the Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires, Argentina, where he continues to serve successfully to this day. His life is now dedicated to teaching his students (Bojrim) the profound philosophy of Chabad Chassidus. Additionally, he advises them daily on matters related to their service to Hashem and spiritual development.

Rab. Zalmen Farkash

Mashpia of Yeshiva
Maguid Shiur Mesivte

Born in Jerusalem, he began his studies at the Mesivte of Lod. To further his Yeshiva studies, he moved to Montreal, Canada, where he spent five years completing his studies as a Bocher. He served as Shliach in New Haven, Connecticut, and was Mashpia and Maguid Shiur in Brunoy, Paris. After marrying, he moved to Argentina, where he now serves as Mashpia at the Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires and Maguid Shiur at the Mesivte of Buenos Aires.

Rab. Mordechai Srugo

Meishiv of Yeshiva

Born in Tigre, Buenos Aires Province, Argentina, he studied in his youth with his father, Rabbi Abraham Srugo AHS. At the age of 18, he moved to New York, where he studied at the Yeshiva Gedolah of Central Chabad. After his marriage, he continued his studies at the Kollel Nachalat Har Chabad in Kiryat Malachi, Israel, and later at the Kollel in Jerusalem.
He had the merit and honor of founding the first Kollel in Mexico and becoming the first Shochet in the country. In 1986, with the special blessing of the Rebbe, he moved to Argentina, where he has been serving as an educator at Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires to this day. People who know him well know that since his childhood in Chinuch, Rabbi Srugo has always had a book in his hands.

Rab. Shmuel Schtroks

Rosh Yeshiva

Born in London, England, he studied at the Mesivte and Yeshiva Gedolah of Lubavitch in his hometown. He continued his studies in France, culminating them as a Bocher at the Yeshiva Oholei Toire with Rabbi Israel Fridman. He was on Shlichut in Kfar Chabad. He served as Maguid Shiur at the Yeshiva Ktana in Moscow and the Yeshiva Gedolah in Frankfurt, Germany. He currently serves as Rosh Yeshiva at the Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires.

Rab. Lipe Baumgarten

Mashgiach of Yeshiva

Born in Buenos Aires, he developed his studies at Oholey Chinuch and the Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires. His studies continued in Kiryat Gat, Israel, and Montreal, Canada. He spent two years on Shlichut in Melbourne, Australia. He now lives with his wife and children in Buenos Aires, where he serves as Mashgiach at Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires.

Rab. Moshe Israel Memun

Maguid Shiur of Yeshiva

Born in Buenos Aires, Argentina, he completed his initial studies at the Cheder and Yeshiva Ktana in Shuba, Israel. He moved to New York to complete his studies at the Yeshiva Tora Be Irha. Upon returning to Buenos Aires, he continued his studies at the Kollel Shuba Israel. His teaching career began by preparing Chatanim and offering courses at the Kollel for Balebatim.
He has been serving as Maguid Shiur at Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires for 30 years.

Rab. Dovber Baumgarten

General Mashpia of Mesivte

Born in the USA, he moved to Buenos Aires, Argentina, in his childhood, where he began his studies at Yeshiva Gedolah of Buenos Aires. He continued his studies as a Bocher in Kiryat Gat, Israel, and in Montreal, Canada. He completed his studies at Yeshiva 770 and graduated as a Rabbi (Smicha) with Rabbi Itzchok Yeroslavsky.
